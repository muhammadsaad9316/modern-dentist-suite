# Dentist Trust-First Website Design Workflow

## Objective
Design dentist websites that immediately build trust, reduce anxiety, and convert visitors into booked appointments using psychology-driven layout, calm visual design, and frictionless UX.

The agent must prioritize **patient confidence over visual flash**.

---

## Core Principles
- Trust before beauty
- Clarity before creativity
- Humans before technology
- Booking before browsing

---

## Phase 1: First Impression (0–5 seconds)

### Rules
- Use real clinic or dentist imagery (never generic stock models)
- Show a human face immediately
- Avoid aggressive animations
- Use calm color palette (white, light gray, soft blue/mint)

### Mandatory Hero Structure
1. Dentist or clinic image
2. Clear benefit-based headline
3. Primary CTA: “Book Appointment”

### Headline Formula
> Gentle + Modern Dental Care + Location or Key Benefit

---

## Phase 2: Immediate Trust Signals (5–30 seconds)

### Required Elements
- Years of experience
- Certifications or professional standards
- Modern equipment indicators
- Hygiene & safety reassurance

### Layout Rules
- Use icon-based blocks
- Max 2 lines per item
- No medical jargon
- No marketing buzzwords

---

## Phase 3: Emotional Reassurance (Fear Reduction)

### Goal
Address dental anxiety and fear of pain or loss of control.

### Required Content
- Explicit acknowledgment of fear
- Patient control reassurance
- Calm, empathetic language

### Design Rules
- Rounded corners
- Increased white space
- Softer contrast than hero section

---

## Phase 4: Authority Through Human Story

### Dentist Profile Rules
- Professional portrait
- Short personal story, not CV
- Focus on care philosophy

### Story Structure
1. Why I chose dentistry
2. How I treat patients
3. What patients can expect emotionally

---

## Phase 5: Social Proof

### Required Proof Types
- Google review rating
- Short patient testimonials
- Emotional outcomes preferred over technical praise

### Rules
- Real names or initials
- No fake testimonials
- No overly long reviews

---

## Phase 6: Booking Experience (Conversion Core)

### Booking UX Rules
- Max 3 steps
- No account required
- Mobile-first
- Clear confirmation feedback

### Mandatory Trust Copy Near CTA
- “Takes less than 2 minutes”
- “No obligation”
- “Your data is protected”

---

## Phase 7: Final Reassurance Footer

### Required Elements
- Emergency contact
- Accepted insurance logos
- Languages spoken
- Hygiene or safety badges
- Clear clinic address & map

---

## Prohibited Patterns
- Loud colors
- Sales language
- Long paragraphs
- Popups before trust is built
- Auto-playing videos with sound

---

## Success Criteria
A visitor should:
1. Feel safe within 5 seconds
2. Trust the dentist within 30 seconds
3. Understand how to book within 1 minute
4. Complete booking within 2–4 minutes

---

## Output Expectation
When applying this workflow, the agent should produce:
- Clear section hierarchy
- Calm, premium visual direction
- Copy that reduces fear
- A booking-focused layout
